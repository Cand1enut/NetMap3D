The loop

From site survey to signed-off install.

01

Plan

Sketch the logical network in 2D — a flowchart that stays live-linked to the physical build.

02

Build

Rooms, floors, racks, and real products — UniFi, Cisco, Aruba and more — placed the way they actually mount.

03

Walk

First-person, game-grade controls. Drill Ø ¾" holes, route cable with real physics, zip-tie the bundles.

04

Install

Your tech opens the map on a tablet: exact ports, exact lengths, exact holes. VLANs simulated and verified.

Install-grade fidelity

Everything in the map exists 1:1 in the model.

Not an "essence" of a switch — the switch. True port layouts with WAN and SFP+ where they really are, link LEDs lit in cable colors, rear power inlets corded to real UPS outlets, patch panels with front jacks and rear punchdowns.

Port-level truth

Every jack is clickable, numbered, VLAN-aware, and validated. Blocked links glow red in simulation.

Real cat6 physics

Cables hang, drape, bundle, and collide — nothing clips through walls, racks, or floors.

Real scale

Feet and inches everywhere. Measure anything. A 100 ft wall is 100 ft; a 328 ft run gets flagged.

Built-in analyst

Coverage gaps, unpowered gear, missing failover, over-length runs — caught before the truck rolls.

Together, live

Your whole team in the same building.

Google-Docs-style multiplayer: see teammates' cursors, watch them walk the site, split the rack work, and hand the finished model to installers with a link. (Team plan — rolling out in beta.)
